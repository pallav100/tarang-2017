ABOUT TARANG

We love music

Tarang – a musical extravaganza from the Indian heartland and its innumerably diverse facets. Cover an existing piece, or create your own. Come as a band and the stage is yours.


Registration

  • Pre-registration (includes video submission). Team participation.

  • Submit a 2-3 minute long video showcasing your team’s practice routine/previous performances to events@bits-oasis.org and cc the same to pcr@bits-oasis.org.

  • “ - Oasis ’17 Video Submission”.

  • For last date for submission, check registration details.

  • The selected teams, once on-campus should report to the Department of Theatre room (2214, FD-II) with their IDs post registration at the Controls booth.

RULES

No fun without rules

    ROUND I-Tarang


  • Bands can perform any songs of their choice, cover or an original composition.

  • Only the top three teams from this round can participate in the Equals Fusion Music Night round.

  • Maximum of 10 members and minimum of 3 members per band. The two teams could have a maximum of 3 common band members.

  • Total Time Limit: 20 minutes (5 minutes for the sound check and 15 minutes for the performance.)

  • If a team exceeds the time allocated for sound check, the extra time taken shall be reduced from the performance time. An indicative announcement will be made at the time limit of 20 minutes to notify the judges about the extension.

  • 5% of the total marks would be deducted for every extra minute. Sounds would be cut after 23 minutes.

  • All members of the band need not be of the same college. Bands can have team members from other colleges also.

  • All the members should be registered students with a valid college ID of the ongoing academic tenure of the college.

  • Use of pre-recorded music (e.g., sequences, keyboard samples, guitar/octapad loops) will result in disqualification.

  • The songs should have lyrics from any Indian language. However, a refrain with English lyrics is allowed.

  • Purely classical numbers are not allowed. However, a classical refrain is allowed

  • Bands should bring their own instruments. Drum kit and sounds equipment will be provided by BITS Pilani.


    ROUND II - Equals Fusion Music Night


    This event is to showcase that music has no language or boundaries. BITS Pilani is proud to debut a new platform where musicians from various genres and cultures would collaborate to produce new music! Imagine those magical instances when music was created when you first met your band members! Anahad gives an opportunity to collaborate with unique instruments and folk musicians of India and create something unique. We invite musicians across genres, whether WESTERN/EASTERN or METAL/FOLK to take this challenge and showcase the limitless power of Music!



  • Only the top three teams from the previous round will participate in the Equals Fusion Music Night round.

  • The finalist teams will be allocated a practice room for a period of 48 hours to collaborate with the reputed folk artists from Jaiselmer provided by Anahad Foundation.

  • Each team will be provided with a drumkit and required amps.

  • The bands along with the folk artists have to prepare a fusion 20-minute performance which they will present in the Equals Fusion Music Night.

  • The teams have the choice to only go ahead with few members from their original contingent for Tarang, as long as there are at least 3 band members contributing.

  • Total Time Limit: 20 minutes

  • If a team exceeds the time allocated for sound check, the extra time taken shall be reduced from the performance time. An indicative announcement will be made at the time limit of 20 minutes to notify the judges about the extension.

  • Use of pre-recorded music (e.g., sequences, keyboard samples, guitar/octapad loops) will result in disqualification

  • The songs should have lyrics from any Indian language. However, a refrain (few repetitive lines) with English lyrics is allowed.

  • Purely classical numbers are not allowed. However, a classical refrain is allowed.

  • The teams will be performing in the open air amphitheater, Rotunda of BITS Pilani and will be a part of the Equals Fusion Music Night prof show.


    Judging Criteria

  • Creativity

  • Quality of vocals and instrumental music

  • Tightness and synchronization

  • Choice of song (in case of cover; composition otherwise)

  • Overall impact
